Low Poly Scene Cel Shading

Cel Shader, Stylized Water Shader and Unity Scene Rendering

I rendered a low-poly, cartoon-shaded style scene in Unity, with the entire art production process all done myself. Below, I will describe the process of creating this scene, from modeling to shading and rendering in Unity.

Modeling

First, I used Blender to model all the artistic assets within the scene, exporting them in FBX format for import into Unity.

Shading

Following this, I developed shaders in Unity, focusing primarily on a cel shader and a shader for stylized water materials. In the cel shader, I manually calculated the dot product between the light direction and the surface normals. I then used the SmoothStep function to create sharp, flat shadows, enhancing the scene's pseudo-2.5D appearance.

For the stylized water shader, I implemented three key features:

Refraction, adjustable via the "IOR" parameter.

Water texture, which can be modified by altering the normal map and distortion parameters.

Transparency, controllable through the Fade parameter.

Unity Rendering Adjustment

Finally, I fine-tuned the rendering settings in Unity, adjusting lighting, shadow rendering methods, and the background. I also applied post-processing effects using the Global Volume to enhance the 2.5D look, making the colors more vibrant and the overall scene more visually appealing.
